About RC WoundCare

RC WoundCare is a trusted distributor of premium allograft products for critical wound care cases, offering nationwide coverage to leading physicians, hospitals, and surgery centers across the United States. We provide a comprehensive line of human allografts for use in surgical specialties such as spine, orthopedics, and wound care.

Partnering with top-tier manufacturing facilities, RC WoundCare ensures that all products are processed with the highest standards of sterility and safety, using certified, fully validated cleanroom environments and strict aseptic techniques.

Why Amniotic Tissue?

The use of amniotic membrane as a wound covering has been documented in clinical practice for over 110 years, with more than 100 publications highlighting its various potential benefits. Amniotic membrane contains essential growth factors and offers a natural human tissue scaffold, which, according to published literature, provides numerous advantages as a wound covering. It is widely utilized as a biological covering in a broad range of surgical and wound care applications.

Donor Selection & Screening

All donors are required to meet strict acceptance criteria and undergo a comprehensive evaluation by the Medical Director. This includes an interview with the donor's next of kin and a thorough review of all relevant medical records. Each donor is subjected to an intensive screening process, including serological testing, to ensure patient safety. Donors are individually assessed to verify the absence of any disqualifying conditions.

All screening and eligibility protocols comply with FDA 21 CFR 1271 regulations and adhere to the standards set by the American Association of Tissue Banks (AATB) for each HCT/P product type.

Patient Reassurance Program

The Patient Reassurance Program provides statement credit for the patient when a patient claim is denied after receiving a positive coverage response from the Stability Biologics Reimbursement Hotline. The intention of this program is to provide assistance to patients such that they will not be charged for grafts that have gone through the Stability Biologics Hotline Reassurance program but are subsequently denied payment through CMS or their insurance.

This reimbursement will only be for the value of the implanted graft and may be provided to cover a patients bill for that graft or on behalf of the patient in the form of a credit to the Physician Clinic/Hospital Outpatient Clinic.

Place of Service

Office

Location other than a hospital, skilled nursing facility (SNF), military treatment facility, community health center, State or Local public health clinic, or intermediate care facility (ICF), where the health professional routinely provides health examinations, diagnosis, and treatment of illness or injury on an ambulatory basis.

Home

Location, other than a hospital or other facility, where the patient receives care in a private residence.

Nursing facility

A facility which primarily provides to residents skilled nursing care and related services for the rehabilitation of injured, disabled, or sick persons, or, on a regular basis, health-related care services above the level of custodial care to other than individuals with intellectual disabilities.

Inpatient hospital

A facility, other than psychiatric, which primarily provides diagnostic, therapeutic (both surgical and nonsurgical), and rehabilitation services by, or under, the supervision of physicians to patients admitted for a variety of medical conditions.

Outpatient hospital

A portion of a hospital's main campus which provides diagnostic, therapeutic (both surgical and nonsurgical), and rehabilitation services to sick or injured persons who do not require hospitalization or institutionalization. Description change effective January 1, 2016.

Ambulatory surgical center

A freestanding facility, other than a physician's office, where surgical and diagnostic services are provided on an ambulatory basis.
